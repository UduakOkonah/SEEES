Courses

Our Courses

At SEEES UNICROSS, we offer a variety of courses designed to provide in-depth knowledge and hands-on experience in Electrical and Electronics Engineering. Our courses are tailored to equip students with the skills needed to excel in the field.

First Semester

Introduction to Electrical Engineering

This course covers the basics of electrical engineering, including circuit analysis, electrical systems, and basic electronics. It's designed for beginners and provides a solid foundation in the field.

Basic Mathematics for Engineers

This course focuses on mathematical concepts and techniques used in engineering, including algebra, calculus, and differential equations.

Physics for Engineers

Fundamental principles of physics relevant to engineering, covering mechanics, thermodynamics, and electromagnetism.

Second Semester

Introduction to Programming

An introductory course in programming, covering basic concepts and techniques using a high-level programming language.

Engineering Drawing

Principles and techniques of engineering drawing, including the use of CAD software.

Basic Electrical Measurements

This course introduces students to electrical measurement techniques and instruments.

First Semester

Advanced Electronics

In this course, students dive deeper into electronic components and systems. Topics include semiconductor devices, integrated circuits, and microelectronics.

Signals and Systems

This course covers the analysis of signals and systems, including Fourier and Laplace transforms.

Digital Logic Design

Principles of digital logic design, including combinational and sequential circuits.

Second Semester

Control Systems

This course focuses on the principles and applications of control systems in engineering. Students will learn about feedback systems, stability analysis, and controller design.

Electromagnetic Fields and Waves

Study of electromagnetic fields, waves, and their applications in engineering.

Computer Architecture

This course covers the fundamental concepts of computer architecture, including the design and operation of CPUs and memory systems.

First Semester

Power Systems

Explore the generation, transmission, and distribution of electrical power. This course covers power system analysis, electrical machines, and renewable energy sources.

Communication Systems

This course provides an overview of analog and digital communication systems. Students will learn about modulation techniques, signal processing, and network protocols.

Microprocessors

An in-depth study of microprocessors, including architecture, programming, and interfacing techniques.

Second Semester

Power Electronics

This course covers the principles and applications of power electronics, including converters, inverters, and power supplies.

Embedded Systems

Learn about the design and development of embedded systems. Topics include microcontrollers, real-time operating systems, and interfacing techniques.

Digital Signal Processing

Principles and techniques of digital signal processing, including filtering, sampling, and Fourier analysis.

First Semester

VLSI Design

This course covers the principles and techniques of VLSI (Very Large Scale Integration) design, including CMOS technology and layout design.

Wireless Communication

Study of wireless communication systems, including cellular networks, wireless protocols, and antenna design.

Optoelectronics

This course explores the principles and applications of optoelectronic devices, including LEDs, lasers, and photodetectors.

Second Semester

Robotics and Automation

An introduction to robotics and automation systems, including robot kinematics, sensors, and control.

Renewable Energy Systems

This course covers the principles and applications of renewable energy sources, including solar, wind, and hydro power.

Advanced Control Systems

A deeper exploration of control systems, including modern control theory, state-space analysis, and nonlinear systems.
